Accounting Basics

  • Activities & Users

    Accounting consists of three basic activities. It identifies, records and communicates the economic events of an organization to interested users.

    As a starting point, a company has to identify the economic events relevant to business. Once it identifies the events, it records (or bookkeeps) those events in order to provide a history of its financial activities.

    Finally, the company communicates the collected information to interested users by means of accounting reports.The most popular accounting reports are called financial statements.

    The users of accounting reports can be internal or external users. Internal users need detailed information on a timely basis. Managerial accounting provides internal reports and dashboards to help users make business decisions. External users are primarily investors (who wants to know whether the company is growing its market share) or creditors (who wants to know whether the company will have money to pay off its debts).

  • GAAP

    Generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) refer to a common set of accounting rules, standards, and procedures issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB). Public companies in the U.S. must follow GAAP when their accountants compile their financial statements.

    The Securities Exchange Commission is the agency of the U.S Government that overseas U.S. financial markets and accounting standard setting bodies. SEC relies on FASB to set the standards.

    Companies outside of the U.S. have adopted the accounting standards issued by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB). These standards are called International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). In the recent years, both the standard setting bodies have made efforts to reduce the differences between GAAP and IFRS. This process is referred to as convergence.

My All Time Favorites

The Black Swan

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

This book illuminated the significant role of randomness in our lives. It helped me appreciate the profound impact of rare, high-impact events, often overlooked due to their low probability.

Atlas Shrugged

Ayn Rand

Although I don't agree with all the protagonists' whims in this book, it has empowered me to live life on my own terms and avoid second-hand egoism.

Thinking, Fast and Slow

Daniel Kahneman

This book enabled me to make conscious and intentional decisions. It also helped me identify heuristic decisions arising from System 1 thinking in others.

The Years of Lyndon Johnson

Robert A Caro

This is how history should be writtenâ€”engaging, entertaining, and educational. This book captivated my imagination so much that I often found myself concerned about Mr. Caro's health during COVID outbreak.

The Great War for Civilisation

Robert Fisk

A much-needed primer on Middle Eastern history, politics, and ongoing crises. Mr. Fisk's firsthand accounts can easily rival any Hollywood thriller.

The Blind Watchmaker

Richard Dawkins

You'll feel the theory of evolution by natural selection in the marrow of your bones. Darwin would be proud of Mr. Dawkins' work.

Basic Economics

Thomas Sowell

Economics, as an emergent phenomenon, isn't instinctive. This book demystifies it, making it essential for anyone living in the modern economy and aspiring to make the most of scarce and valuable resources.

Animal Farm

George Orwell

This book unmasks false altruism and reveals how the unearned power usurps resources from the hardworking to the hardly working in totalitarian regimes

Life 3.0

Max Tegmark

An excellent exploration of the diverse pathways through which a world with superhuman artificial intelligence may come to fruition.

Where Good Ideas Come From

Steven Johnson

The author eloquently narrates the emergence of the often-overlooked aspect of innovative persistence in life. These incremental innovations aren't solely driven by heroes but rather by a network of agents working in tandem.

Algorithms to Live By

Brian Christian

This book is essential reading for every computer scientist. With a proper understanding, it unlocks a treasure trove of optimization techniques, enriching both engineering systems and lives alike.

Lord of the Rings

J.R.R. Tolkien

It transports you to the mesmerizing world of Middle Earth with every read. The meticulously crafted characters and environments make me feel as though I've personally visited the Prancing Pony inn alongside Frodo and friends.

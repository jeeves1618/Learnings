Platform Thinking

What is a platform? A platform is a business based on enabling value-creating interactions between external producers and consumers. The platform provides an open, participative infrastructure for these interactions and sets governance conditions for them. The platform’s overarching purpose: to consummate matches among users and facilitate the exchange of goods, services, or social currency, thereby enabling value creation for all participants.

Platform vs Pipeline
  1. Platforms beat pipelines because platforms unlock new sources of value creation and supply.
  2. Platforms beat pipelines because platforms scale more efficiently by eliminating gatekeepers.
  3. Platforms beat pipelines by using data-based tools to create community feedback loops.
  4. Platforms invert the firm. Because the bulk of a platform’s value is created by its community of users, the platform business must shift its focus from internal activities to external activities.
  5. Platforms focussess on demand economies of scale. Pipeline focusses on supply economies of scale.
Network Effects

Network effects refers to the impact that the number of users of a platform has on the value created for each user. Positive network effects refers to the ability of a large, well-managed platform community to produce significant value for each user of the platform. Negative network effects refers to the possibility that the growth in numbers of a poorly-managed platform community can reduce the value produced for each user.

Frictionless Entry Platforms which permit frictionless entry are able to grow organically almost without bound. Frictionless entry is the ability of users to quickly and easily join a platform and begin participating in the value creation that the platform facilitates.

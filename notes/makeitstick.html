Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning

Chapter 1: "Learning is Misunderstood"

This chapter introduces the book's main thesis: that many commonly used study methods are ineffective, even though they feel productive. Strategies like rereading, highlighting, and cramming lead to short-term gains but don’t support long-term retention. The authors argue that more effortful learning strategies, even if they feel harder, are much more effective.

Chapter 2: "To Learn, Retrieve"

The chapter emphasizes retrieval practice (actively recalling information) as a key to solidifying knowledge. Techniques like self-testing or teaching others are shown to improve long-term retention by forcing learners to retrieve information, which strengthens memory pathways. Retrieval practice is described as more beneficial than passive techniques like rereading or reviewing notes.

Chapter 3: "Mix Up Your Practice"

Here, the authors discuss interleaved and varied practice —alternating between different types of problems or subjects within a study session. This contrasts with blocked practice, where a single topic is focused on repeatedly. Interleaving feels more challenging but improves problem-solving skills, adaptability, and the ability to differentiate between concepts.

Chapter 4: "Embrace Difficulties"

This chapter introduces the concept of desirable difficulties, meaning that certain learning strategies, although challenging, lead to better retention. Techniques like spacing, interleaving, and generation (attempting to answer before learning the correct answer) make learning harder but result in stronger memory formation.

Chapter 5: "Avoid Illusions of Knowing"

The authors explore common cognitive biases and illusions that trick people into thinking they know more than they do. For instance, re-reading gives a sense of familiarity but doesn’t build deep understanding. Testing yourself, spacing, and explaining concepts aloud can combat these illusions and ensure genuine comprehension.

Chapter 6: "Get Beyond Learning Styles"

The book challenges the popular notion of learning styles, such as visual, auditory, or kinesthetic. Research shows that matching study techniques to these styles has little effect on learning outcomes. Instead, using proven methods like retrieval practice, interleaved practice, and spacing works across all learners, regardless of preferred style.

Chapter 7: "Increase Your Abilities"

This chapter discusses the concept of growth mindset—the belief that abilities can be developed with effort. People who see intelligence and skills as malleable are more resilient in the face of challenges and perform better academically. The authors highlight that adopting a growth mindset enhances motivation and learning outcomes.

Chapter 8: "Make It Stick"

In the final chapter, the authors summarize the key principles of effective learning:

  • Retrieval practice: Regularly test your recall of information.
  • Spacing: Spread out learning sessions over time.
  • Interleaving: Mix up topics to improve understanding.
  • Embrace difficulties: Choose challenging study techniques that make learning effortful.
  • Feedback and reflection: Regularly assess and refine learning strategies.
They emphasize the importance of applying these techniques consistently to improve long-term memory retention, creativity, and problem-solving abilities.

Summary of Key Takeaways:

Make It Stick advocates for learning techniques that are supported by cognitive psychology. Rather than relying on intuition or common methods, the book suggests:

  • Prioritizing retrieval, spacing, interleaving, and embracing challenges.
  • Discarding myths like learning styles and embracing a growth mindset.
  • Using self-assessment and reflection to improve learning effectiveness.
By implementing these evidence-based strategies, learners can achieve deeper and longer-lasting knowledge retention.

Here is the important tenet of public speaking – tell them what you are going to tell them, tell them, then tell them what you have told them.

Sometimes the only way to proceed in pursuit of good partnership is to decide that whoever cares more about the issue can have their way.

You cannot establish any standard of excellence without criticism. So thoughtful approach to how you correct people must be a part of your culture. Praise is affirmation; criticism is investment.

If your response to criticism is consistently defensive, people are eventually going to stop coming to you with notes, you have made it too unpleasant for them to continue and they are going to stop investing in you, and you are going to stop growing as a result.

Behavioral science expert Rory Sutherland says the opposite of a good idea should also be a good idea. That’s why the idea of unreasonable hospitality was so comforting. The opposite of unreasonable hospitality is reasonable hospitality, and not treating people poorly.

Luxury means giving more; hospitality is being more thoughtful.

Gifts are a way to tell people you saw, heard, and recognize them – that you cared enough to listen and to do something with what you heard. A gift transforms an interaction, taking it from transactional to relational.

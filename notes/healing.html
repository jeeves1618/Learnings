A Shallow Recap Disguised as a Modern Guide

Healing the Modern Brain by Drew Ramsey
⭐️⭐️ (2/5)

The author repeatedly emphasizes the importance of nutrition, sleep, and social connection — pillars of well-being that are certainly important, but hardly “modern” or revelatory. If you're new to this territory and haven’t read Outlive, Why We Sleep, or Social, you might find value here. But for anyone even mildly familiar with recent literature on psychology, mental health, or lifestyle science, this book feels redundant and unnecessarily elaborate.

Worse, Ramsey touches on crucial ideas like neuroplasticity, inflammation, microbiomes, and even psychotherapy, but never dives deep. For example, his mention of psychotherapy relies on Freud’s oft-quoted definition of making the unconscious conscious — a throwaway line that’s never meaningfully expanded. These concepts form the supposed foundation of his thesis, but they're left disconnected, underdeveloped, and frustratingly vague.

My biggest gripe? There is nothing “modern” in Healing the Modern Brain. It completely sidesteps the true challenges of our time — the psychological toll of living in an AI-saturated world, the identity dissonance fueled by social media, and the chronic overstimulation of digital life. Instead, it recycles familiar advice about diet, rest, and social ties without offering new insights or practical frameworks tailored to today’s unique pressures.

In short, this book may work for someone just beginning to explore mental health. But if you're looking for depth, originality, or a genuine engagement with what it means to heal today’s brain, look elsewhere.
